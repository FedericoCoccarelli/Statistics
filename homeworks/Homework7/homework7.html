Research

1- Try to understand the general idea of the Lebesgue-Stieltjes integral and why it is useful concept and notation in Theory of Probability

2- Explain in your own words the "Law of large numbers" and sketch a simple proof (Markov inequality, Čebyšëv inequality, ... )

Before talking about the law of large number we firstly need to intruduce two important theorems: the Markov Inequality and the Chebyshev Inequality.

Markov Inequality
The Markov inequality states that given any non-negative random variable $X$ and a generic $a > 0$ $$ P(X \ge a)\le \frac{E[X]}{a} $$ Proof
Assuming $X$ continuous and being $f$ it's density: $$ E[X] = \int_{0} ^{\infty} x f(x) dx = \int_{0} ^{a} x f(x) dx + \int_{a} ^{\infty} x f(x) dx $$ $$ \ge \int_{a} ^{\infty} xf(x) dx \ge \int_{a} ^{\infty} af(x) dx = a\int_{a} ^{\infty} f(x) dx$$ $$ a P(X \ge a) $$ Dividing all by $a$ we obtain : $$ P(X \ge a)\le \frac{E[X]}{a} $$

Chebyshev Inequality
If $X$ is a random variable with mean $\mu$ and variance $\sigma^2$, then for any $r>0$: $$P(|X - \mu|\ge r)\le \frac{\sigma^2}{r^2}$$ Proof
The proof easily follows from the Markov inequality. In fact, if $X$ is a random variable, then $|X - \mu|$ is a non-negative random variable. Hence, for this new random variable the Markov inequality is valid and then $\forall A > 0$ : $$P(|X-\mu|^2 \ge A)|le \frac{E(|X-\mu|^2)}{A}$$ but $E(|X-\mu|^2)$ is the definition of variance so we obtain: $$P(|X - \mu|\ge r)\le \frac{\sigma^2}{r^2}$$
